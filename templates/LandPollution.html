The Risks of Land Pollution

There are a variety of factors that generate land pollution. These factors range from poor waste management to overcrowded landfills. The most common contaminants are wastes and household garbage. Household garbage includes paper, wood, plastics, rubber, leather, and textiles while common forms of wastes include industrial and commercial wastes. Some industrial waste can be considered hazardous when poorly managed since some hold properties that can potentially harm the environment.

Land pollution is one of the major types of pollution that is responsible for an onslaught of issues. It is responsible for contributing to climate change which often leads to other disastrous issues such as flooding.
